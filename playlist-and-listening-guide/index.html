Dear Diary

    “Dear Diary” — words written over and over again, signaling the start of a story, inked onto the top of a page, with much more to follow. These words are the classic start to all introspective journaling. Sitting down after a long, difficult day, you flip open your book and put pen to paper, ready to feel the cathartic release of your thoughts. Diaries are relics for the passage of time, containing the inner truth of who a person really is. 

     When first listening to “Don’t Forget Me” by Maggie Rogers, the lyrics made me feel like lying on the floor and staring aimlessly up at the ceiling. They were a direct visualization into Rogers' brain, and made me feel like I was actually getting to know Rogers as a person. They were so emotionally charged and personal, that it almost felt like something I shouldn’t hear, like I was intruding on Rogers’ privacy. Upon further reflection, it felt like I had just read her diary. In a way, writing an autobiographical song is no different from writing a diary entry: it takes the same emotional vulnerability, it is a letter without being sent to anyone in particular, and it is a way to make the author feel better and process what is going on in their life.

--

     Within this playlist, I identified six other songs that invoked a similar vibe. These songs are all innately personal, often dropping specific person or place names within their stories. Since flipping through the pages of a diary is essentially flipping through a timeline of one’s life, I have chosen to structure the playlist as such. I have associated each song with a specific stage of life and an approximate age, assigning this to be the story of the impersonal pronoun, “you”; as one song ends and another starts, another row of candles are added to the metaphorical cake.
